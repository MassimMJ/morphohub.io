About Morpho Hub

MorphoHub is an platform we've been developing to help centralize and standardize Computed Tomography (CT) data within the biological sciences. We've noticed that as CT use grows globally, digital collections can sometimes be a bit fragmented. Our goal with MorphoHub is to offer a more connected space, hoping to support research and highlight scientific data from around the world.

Our Key Focus Areas

Specialized Digital Curation

We're working to ensure that CT data is organized and standardized, following established international guidelines like Darwin Core. This helps make sure the information is complete and can be easily found and used by researchers everywhere, aiming for better data integrity and compatibility.

Collaborative Business Model

MorphoHub includes a marketplace feature that we hope will be helpful for both institutions and individual researchers. We envision universities and research centers with CT equipment (like micro-CT scanners) being able to offer their scanning and data processing services (such as segmentation using specialized software). Our hope is that this can help institutions utilize their equipment more effectively. For researchers who might not have access to these facilities, they could potentially connect with specialized technicians for services like 3D rendering or quantitative analysis, which could assist their morphological studies.

Legal Compliance

We're committed to building MorphoHub with respect for legal and ethical standards. This involves considering relevant regulations for accessing biodiversity data, aiming for compliance with international agreements like the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD) and the Nagoya Protocol, and being mindful of paleontological heritage laws in various places. We're also working on a system for managing access to sensitive materials and recording the origin of digitized specimens, always striving for ethical and lawful operations.
